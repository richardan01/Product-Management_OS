# Step 2: Draft Template

## Email Structure

```
Subject: Product Update - Week of [Month Day]

---

## TL;DR
- [Most important outcome/metric this week]
- [Second most important thing]
- [Any critical blocker - skip if none]

## Metrics Snapshot
- Teacher Activation: X% (+/-X% WoW, target X%)
- MRR: $XXK (+/-$XK WoW)
- Trial Conversion: X% (target X%)
- [Additional metric if notable]

## What Shipped
- **[Feature Name]**: [One sentence on what it does and expected impact]
- **[Feature Name]**: [One sentence]

## In Progress
- **[Project Name]**: [Status and expected completion]
- **[Project Name]**: [Status]

## Blockers & Risks
- **[Blocker]**: [Description] → Next step: [Action]
- [Or: "No critical blockers this week"]

## Next Week Focus
1. [Priority 1 - tied to OKR]
2. [Priority 2]
3. [Priority 3]

---
Questions? Reply here or grab 15 min: [calendar link]
```

## Section Guidelines

### TL;DR
- Max 3 bullets
- Lead with wins, but don't hide problems
- If a metric is red, mention it here

### Metrics Snapshot
- Use consistent format every week
- Show directional change (arrows or +/-)
- Include target for context

### What Shipped
- Only include customer-facing or significant internal changes
- One sentence max per item
- Skip bug fixes unless significant

### In Progress
- Top 2-3 active projects only
- Include ETA if known
- Be honest about delays

### Blockers & Risks
- Always include a proposed next step
- Escalate early, not late
- If no blockers, say so explicitly

### Next Week Focus
- 3 items max
- Should connect to current OKRs
- Be specific enough to be held accountable

## Tone Guidelines

- Confident but not arrogant
- Honest about challenges, optimistic about solutions
- Data-forward, opinion-light
- Assume reader has 2 minutes max
