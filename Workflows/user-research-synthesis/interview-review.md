# Step 1: Interview Review

## Goal

Extract key observations from each interview while preserving context and nuance.

## Per-Interview Process

### 1. Read Through Once
- Read the full transcript without highlighting
- Get a sense of the overall story
- Note your initial impressions

### 2. Second Pass: Extract Highlights

Pull out:
- **Direct quotes** that capture key insights (use exact words)
- **Behaviors** described (what they actually do, not what they say they'd do)
- **Pain points** and frustrations
- **Workarounds** they've created
- **Emotional moments** (excitement, frustration, confusion)
- **Surprising statements** that challenge assumptions

### 3. Tag with Themes

As you review, tag highlights with emerging themes:
- Use short, descriptive labels
- It's okay if themes evolve across interviews
- Don't force-fit into predetermined categories

Example tags:
- `time-pressure`
- `grading-anxiety`
- `student-feedback-quality`
- `admin-burden`
- `workaround-spreadsheets`

### 4. Capture Participant Context

For each participant, note:
- Role and experience level
- School type (public/private, size, demographics)
- How long using GradeFlow
- Primary use case
- Tech comfort level

## Interview Summary Format

After reviewing each interview, create a brief summary:

```markdown
## [Participant ID] - [Role]

**Context:** [1-2 sentences on who they are and their situation]

**Key Quotes:**
- "[Exact quote]" - re: [topic]
- "[Exact quote]" - re: [topic]

**Main Pain Points:**
- [Pain point 1]
- [Pain point 2]

**Workarounds/Current Behavior:**
- [What they do today]

**Themes:** #tag1 #tag2 #tag3

**Surprise/Notable:** [Anything unexpected]
```

## Tips for Good Extraction

**Do:**
- Preserve the participant's actual words
- Note the context around quotes
- Include body language/tone if relevant (from notes)
- Capture behaviors, not just opinions

**Don't:**
- Paraphrase too heavily (lose nuance)
- Only pull quotes that support your hypothesis
- Ignore contradictions within a single interview
- Skip the "boring" parts - patterns emerge from mundane details

## Handling Different Interview Types

### Exploratory Interviews
- Cast a wide net with themes
- Pay attention to topics that come up unprompted
- Note what questions generated the most engagement

### Evaluative Interviews (testing a concept)
- Track reactions at specific moments
- Note comprehension issues
- Capture comparison to current behavior

### Problem Interviews
- Focus on frequency and severity of pain
- Understand the full job-to-be-done
- Map the current workflow step-by-step
