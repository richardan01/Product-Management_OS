# User Research Synthesis - Workflow Spec

## Purpose

Transform raw interview data into structured insights that drive product decisions. Good synthesis turns "we talked to users" into "here's what we learned and what to do about it."

## Process Overview

```
Step 1: Review Each Interview (15 min)
    ↓
Step 2: Extract Patterns (15 min)
    ↓
Step 3: Write Synthesis (15 min)
    ↓
Step 4: Share & Discuss
```

## Prerequisites

Before starting synthesis:
- [ ] All interviews completed and transcribed/noted
- [ ] Clear research question documented
- [ ] Understand what decisions this research will inform

## Step 1: Review Each Interview

See `interview-review.md` for full details.

For each interview:
1. Read through transcript/notes
2. Highlight key quotes and observations
3. Tag with emerging themes
4. Note participant context (role, experience level, use case)

## Step 2: Extract Patterns

See `pattern-extraction.md` for full details.

Across all interviews:
1. Cluster similar themes
2. Count frequency (how many participants mentioned each theme)
3. Note contradictions and edge cases
4. Identify strongest patterns (mentioned by 4+ of 6 participants)

## Step 3: Write Synthesis

See `synthesis-template.md` for the output format.

Structure:
1. Executive summary (3-5 bullets)
2. Research context
3. Key findings (with evidence strength)
4. Opportunities
5. Recommendations
6. Appendix (participant details, methodology)

## Step 4: Share & Discuss

- Share synthesis with product team async first
- Schedule 30-min discussion for questions and alignment
- Document decisions made based on findings
- Update roadmap/backlog as appropriate

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing synthesis:
- [ ] Each finding has supporting quotes from multiple participants
- [ ] Disconfirming evidence is acknowledged
- [ ] Participant count is clear (e.g., "5 of 6 teachers mentioned...")
- [ ] Findings are tied to specific opportunities
- [ ] Recommendations are actionable and specific
- [ ] No identifying information if participants requested anonymity

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | How to Avoid |
|---------|--------------|
| Confirmation bias | Actively look for disconfirming evidence |
| Over-generalizing | Always include participant counts |
| Losing nuance | Preserve verbatim quotes, not just summaries |
| No action | Tie each finding to a specific decision or opportunity |
| Recency bias | Weight all interviews equally, not just the last one |
