# Grading Consistency Interview Guide

## Research Objectives
1. Understand how grading inconsistency manifests in practice
2. Identify current workarounds and their effectiveness
3. Gauge willingness to adopt new processes/tools

---

## Target Participants

| Segment | # Interviews | Focus |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Professors (large courses) | 4 | Detection, management, student complaints |
| TAs (experienced) | 4 | Calibration, uncertainty, workload |
| TAs (new) | 3 | Training, confidence, when to ask for help |
| Department admins | 2 | Policies, grade disputes, quality assurance |

---

## Interview Script

### Opening (5 min)
- Thanks for your time
- Recording consent
- Brief intro to research (understanding grading challenges, not selling anything)

### Current State (15 min)

**For Professors:**
1. Walk me through how grading works in your largest course
2. How many TAs? How do you divide the work?
3. Have you ever discovered that TAs were grading differently? How?
4. What happened when students complained about unfair grading?
5. What do you do currently to ensure consistency?

**For TAs:**
1. How were you trained to grade for this course?
2. When you're unsure about a grade, what do you do?
3. Have you ever compared grades with another TA? What did you find?
4. Do you feel confident that your grades are "fair" compared to other TAs?
5. What's the most frustrating part of grading?

### Pain Points (10 min)
1. What's the biggest grading challenge you face?
2. Tell me about a time when grading went wrong
3. If you could wave a magic wand, what would change?

### Solutions Exploration (10 min)
1. What would help you feel more confident about consistency?
2. How would you feel about seeing how your grades compare to others in real-time?
3. Would you use calibration exercises before grading? Why/why not?
4. What would make you NOT want to use a new grading tool?

### Wrap-up (5 min)
1. Anything else I should know?
2. Anyone else I should talk to?
3. OK if I follow up?

---

## Key Quotes to Listen For
- "I had no idea until..."
- "Students always complain about..."
- "I wish I could see..."
- "The problem is..."
- "I try to [workaround]..."
