## Interview Notes

### Current Workflow

Sarah grades using GradeFlow approximately 10-12 hours/week. Her workflow:
1. Checks assignment queue Monday morning
2. Claims submissions from "Unassigned" pool
3. Grades in 2-3 hour blocks between classes
4. Tries to finish her "share" by Wednesday

**Pain point:** No way to know what a "fair share" actually is. She estimates based on dividing total submissions by number of TAs, but other TAs don't seem to follow the same logic.

### Workload Distribution Experience

Sarah described significant frustration with workload equity:

- She consistently grades more than other TAs (her estimate: 40% of work with 25% of TAs)
- One TA in particular "disappears" during midterms/finals when grading volume spikes
- Professor Martinez is unaware of the imbalance
- Sarah doesn't want to "throw anyone under the bus" by complaining

**Current coping:** Sarah has started keeping a personal spreadsheet to track her grading volume. She showed me the spreadsheet - it shows her grading 287 submissions in Week 3 while another TA graded 89.

### Communication with Professor

- Weekly sync meeting (15 min) with all TAs and professor
- Discussion is about "logistics" - due dates, rubric questions
- Workload never directly discussed
- Sarah feels awkward bringing it up in group setting

### What Would Help

When asked what tools would help:

1. **Visibility for professor** - "If Dr. Martinez could just see the numbers, she'd fix it. She's fair, she just doesn't know."

2. **Benchmark data** - "Tell me what's normal. Am I being a perfectionist, or is this actually too much?"

3. **Proactive alerts** - "Flag it before I burn out, not after I complain"

4. **Historical data for reference letters** - "When I ask for recommendations, I want to show I did way more than average"

### Reaction to Workload Dashboard Concept

Showed wireframe concept of professor dashboard. Reaction:

- "Oh wow, this would solve everything"
- Appreciated that it's for professors, not TAs monitoring each other
- Concern: "Would the other TAs know I can see this?" - clarified TAs won't have access in v1
- Suggestion: Eventually give TAs a view of their own stats (not others')

### GradeFlow Feedback (General)

- Loves AI feedback suggestions - saves significant time
- Rubric feature works well
- Would like better mobile experience for quick grading between classes
- Wishes there was a "claim" notification so she knows when work is available

---

## Key Insights

1. **Inequity is invisible** - The professor has no idea workload is imbalanced
2. **TAs won't self-advocate** - Social dynamics prevent raising the issue
3. **Data as fairness** - Sarah wants objective data to replace subjective complaints
4. **Burnout is real** - She's considering reducing TA hours despite needing the income
5. **Trust in professor** - Believes professor would act if given information
