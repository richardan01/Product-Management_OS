## Interview Notes

### Current Workflow

Marcus grades 8-10 hours/week, typically in longer focused sessions:
- Friday afternoons (3-4 hours)
- Sunday evenings (4-5 hours)
- Sometimes Monday mornings if volume is high

He prefers to "batch" his grading rather than spreading it out.

**Pain point:** When he returns to the queue on Friday, it's sometimes nearly empty because someone (he suspects one specific TA) grabbed a lot of submissions Thursday night. Other times it's overflowing. Unpredictable.

### Workload Distribution Experience

Marcus has a more pragmatic view than Sarah, but similar frustrations:

- Estimates he does "about average" - roughly his fair share
- One TA consistently does less - always has "excuses"
- One TA (Sarah, though he doesn't know her by name) does way more
- He thinks the professor should address it but hasn't

**Interesting observation:** Marcus mentioned there's an unspoken "race" to grade the easy submissions. Some TAs grab simple quiz responses, leaving the complex essay questions for others.

> "It's not just volume, it's *what* you grade. Essay questions take 5x longer than multiple choice review."

### Communication with Professor

- Prof. Johnson uses email only - no regular sync meetings
- "Slow grading" emails feel passive-aggressive
- Marcus has never spoken 1:1 with professor about workload
- Doesn't want to seem like he's complaining or slacking

### What Would Help

1. **Assignment-type weighting** - "Count an essay as 5 submissions, not 1"
2. **Professor visibility** - "Just let her see the numbers, she can figure it out"
3. **Queue management** - "Maybe auto-assign so it's forced to be equal?"
4. **Time tracking** - "I'd log my hours if it meant anything"

### Reaction to Workload Dashboard Concept

Showed wireframe concept. Reaction:

- Positive overall, but wanted more nuance
- "Volume is good but time spent would be better"
- Liked the trend view - "you could see if someone disappears during finals week"
- Asked if TAs could see it - when told no, said "that's probably right for now"

**Feature suggestion:** Show not just number of submissions but also assignment types graded. An essay should "count" more than a quiz.

### GradeFlow Feedback (General)

- AI suggestions are "hit or miss" for biology - sometimes suggests feedback that doesn't make sense for the discipline
- Wants better rubric templates for lab reports specifically
- Export to Canvas gradebook is clunky - often has to manually adjust
- Would pay for a mobile app (university doesn't provide one)

---

## Key Insights

1. **Quality vs. quantity** - Volume metrics alone miss the picture; assignment complexity matters
2. **Strategic behavior** - TAs game the system by picking easy submissions
3. **Passive professor communication** - Group emails create tension without solving problems
4. **Auto-assignment interest** - Some TAs would prefer forced equal distribution over optional claiming
5. **Pragmatic acceptance** - Marcus is less emotionally affected but still wants fairness
