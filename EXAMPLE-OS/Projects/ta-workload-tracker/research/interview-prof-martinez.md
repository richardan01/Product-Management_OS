## Interview Notes

### Current TA Management Approach

Prof. Martinez takes a "trust-based" approach:
- Assigns general responsibilities at semester start
- Holds weekly 15-minute syncs (logistics only)
- Checks aggregate grading progress before deadlines
- Addresses issues only if deadlines are missed

**Pain point:** She suspects workload imbalances exist but has no data. By the time issues surface, the semester is nearly over.

### Awareness of Workload Issues

When asked directly about workload distribution:

- Admitted she "probably" has imbalances
- Mentioned one TA (didn't name) who seems "less engaged"
- Another TA seems to "do everything" - she appreciates them but worries about burnout
- Feels guilty that she can't manage this better

**Critical moment:** I mentioned I'd interviewed Sarah (her TA, without identifying her). Prof. Martinez said:

> "Sarah is amazing. I hope she's not doing more than her share. I would feel terrible."

This suggests she'd act on information if she had it.

### What She's Tried

- **Semester debrief conversations** - Asks TAs how it went, but they give vague answers
- **Spreadsheet attempt** - Started manually tracking once, abandoned after 2 weeks
- **Equal assignment pools** - Tried pre-assigning submissions evenly, but TAs complained about inflexibility

Nothing has stuck because everything requires ongoing effort she can't sustain.

### Ideal Solution

Asked what a perfect world would look like:

1. **Passive visibility** - "Don't make me do work. Just show me a dashboard I can glance at."
2. **Alerts for extremes** - "Tell me if it's bad. I don't need to know if it's fine."
3. **Conversation starter** - "Give me something I can share in our meeting. 'Hey, I noticed this pattern...'"
4. **Historical data** - "At the end of the semester, help me write better letters."

### Reaction to Workload Dashboard Concept

Showed wireframe concept. Reaction:

**Very positive.** Key feedback:

- "This is exactly what I need. I could look at this in 20 seconds."
- Loved the bar chart showing relative volumes
- Appreciated the alert concept - "I'd want email, not just in-app"
- Asked about time period selection - weekly is good, but wants semester view too
- Suggested: Show a "fairness score" or simple indicator (green/yellow/red)

**Concern raised:** "Would my TAs see this? I don't want them to feel surveilled."

I explained TAs wouldn't have access in v1. She was relieved but said she might actually *want* to share it with them eventually - "transparency could be healthy."

### GradeFlow Feedback (General)

- "GradeFlow has been transformative for grading quality and consistency"
- Loves AI feedback suggestions - TAs give more detailed feedback now
- Rubric feature improved grade consistency across TAs
- Wants better reports for accreditation purposes (assessment documentation)
- Would love integration with campus SSO (current login is annoying)

---

## Key Insights

1. **Trust-based management fails silently** - Professors don't monitor, problems fester
2. **Time is the blocker** - She would manage better with effortless visibility
3. **Emotional investment in TA wellbeing** - Genuinely cares about fairness, not just outcomes
4. **Alerts >> dashboards** - Proactive notifications more valuable than passive monitoring
5. **Transparency could be positive** - She's open to eventually sharing data with TAs
