# TA Excellence: The Board Narrative

## Document Purpose

This is the story we're telling the board about TA Excellence. The goal is to connect our product work to business outcomes in a compelling, credible way.

---

## The Narrative Arc

### 1. The Strategic Bet (October 2024)

> "We told the board in October that TA management was an underserved opportunity. Professors manage teams of TAs but have no tools to do it well. We bet that solving this would unlock enterprise deals and drive expansion revenue."

**Key message:** We made a strategic choice, not a random feature request.

### 2. The Validation (Q4 2024)

> "In Q4, we validated the bet. We shipped foundational features, ran extensive user research, and confirmed both the problem and our solution direction."

**Evidence:**
- 12 user interviews confirming pain points
- TA Permissions shipped, removing top enterprise blocker
- 45 beta users for TA Performance features
- Pipeline influenced: $450K ARR citing TA features

### 3. The Momentum (Q1 2025)

> "In Q1, we're executing on the vision. The Workload Tracker launches in March and will be our flagship TA feature. Early feedback from design partners is exceptionally strong."

**Evidence:**
- Workload Tracker on track for March 24 GA
- 15 design partners committed to beta
- Competitive moat: no direct competitor has this feature
- NPS for TA features up 4 points in one quarter

### 4. The Path Forward (H1 2025)

> "By end of H1, we'll have a complete TA management suite that differentiates GradeFlow in enterprise deals and creates expansion revenue opportunities at the department level."

**Roadmap:**
- Q1: Workload Tracker GA
- Q2: TA Self-Service, Department Admin View
- H2: Mobile, Advanced Analytics, Predictive Features

## Anticipated Questions & Answers

### Q: "How do you know this will move the needle on revenue?"

**A:** Three data points:
1. $450K in pipeline explicitly cites TA features as decision criteria
2. University of Texas deal ($180K) was unblocked by TA Permissions
3. Department-level features create natural expansion path (avg university has 15+ departments)

### Q: "What's the risk if this doesn't work?"

**A:** Low risk, high upside:
- Investment is 2 engineers (10% of eng)
- Features benefit all users, not just TAs
- Even if expansion doesn't materialize, we've removed enterprise blockers

### Q: "Why will professors actually use this?"

**A:** User research is unambiguous:
- 100% of professors interviewed expressed interest
- They already care about TA welfare - they just lack tools
- Dashboard takes 30 seconds to check - fits existing workflow

### Q: "What about Gradescope? Won't they copy this?"

**A:** They might, but:
- They're 12+ months behind (no public roadmap signals)
- We'll have 2 semesters of customer data and feedback by then
- Our AI grading + TA management creates integrated experience
