## How to Work With David

### In 1:1s

- Come with an agenda (shared doc, he reads before)
- Lead with blockers and decisions needed
- Save "thinking out loud" for end of meeting
- He appreciates when I propose solutions, not just problems
- He's good at career conversations - schedule quarterly

### Getting Decisions

1. Write up options with tradeoffs (2-3 options ideal)
2. Include your recommendation and why
3. Share doc async first, let him read
4. Discuss in 1:1 or quick sync
5. He decides quickly once he has context

**What he looks for:**
- Customer impact clearly articulated
- Engineering input already incorporated
- Risks acknowledged
- Reversibility considered

### Giving Updates

- Weekly written update in our shared 1:1 doc
- Flag risks early (he hates surprises)
- Don't bury bad news - lead with it
- Share wins too - he amplifies to leadership

### When He Pushes Back

- Usually means he sees something I don't
- Ask "help me understand" - he'll explain
- Sometimes he's testing conviction; if I believe it, push back
- When he says "I'm not sure about this," it means no unless convinced
