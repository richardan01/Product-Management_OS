## Interaction Level

- Monthly skip-level 1:1s (30 min)

## Communication Style

**Prefers:**
- Concise, structured communication
- Metrics-first framing
- Strategic context, not tactical details
- Polished presentations for important topics
- Confidence (she can smell uncertainty)

**Dislikes:**
- Waffling or hedging excessively
- Bringing problems without solutions
- Missing the business context
- Surprises in group settings
- Long-winded explanations

**How she processes:**
- Asks sharp questions quickly
- Silence means she's thinking
- "That's interesting" often means skepticism
- She'll interrupt if you're losing her

## How to Present to Maria

### The Format She Likes

1. **One sentence:** What you're asking for / sharing
2. **Context:** Why this matters (30 seconds)
3. **Recommendation:** What you think we should do
4. **Key tradeoffs:** What you considered
5. **Ask:** What you need from her

### Example Structure

> "I'm recommending we delay AI grading for creative writing by one quarter.
>
> Context: Customer research shows accuracy isn't there yet - we're seeing 55% acceptance rate vs. 85% for structured essays. Pushing ahead risks trust damage.
>
> Instead, I recommend we focus Q1 on improving structured essay AI and revisit creative writing in Q2 with more training data.
>
> Tradeoff: We'll miss the marketing moment of 'AI for all assignments,' but we protect our quality reputation.
>
> I need: Your sign-off on this scope change and help communicating to sales."

### Presenting in Product Review

- Know your stuff cold (she will ask detailed questions)
- Have backup slides for likely questions
- Don't read slides; she reads faster than you talk
- Acknowledge uncertainty honestly but confidently
- When she pushes back, listen first, then respond

## What Maria Cares About

**Strategic priorities:**
1. Revenue impact (tied to company goals)
2. Competitive differentiation (are we building moats?)
3. Customer retention (she watches churn closely)
4. Team capability (can we execute on ambition?)

**Questions she often asks:**
- "What's the customer outcome?"
- "How does this move our key metrics?"
- "What are competitors doing here?"
- "Why now? Why not later?"
- "What's the opportunity cost?"

**Pet peeves:**
- Scope creep without re-justification
- Building features because competitors have them
- Ignoring data that contradicts your hypothesis
- Not knowing your numbers

- A question about strategy or her perspective

## How to Build Credibility With Maria

1. **Know your numbers:** She respects PMs who have data fluency
2. **Ship quality:** One polished launch > three rushed ones
3. **Think ahead:** Anticipate her questions before she asks
4. **Be confident:** Not arrogant, but own your perspective
5. **Make David look good:** She notices his team's performance

## Red Flags / How to Lose Her Trust

- Overpromising and underdelivering
- Not knowing customer context when presenting
- Being defensive when challenged
- Going around David inappropriately
- Complaining without taking ownership
