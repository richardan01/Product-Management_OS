# EdTech Trends 2026

*Last updated: January 2026*
*Sources: HolonIQ, Educause Horizon Report, internal research*

## Macro Trends

### 1. AI Goes Mainstream in Assessment

**What's happening:**
- AI grading moved from "experimental" to "expected" in 2025
- 45% of US universities now use some form of AI in assessment (up from 12% in 2024)
- Major LMS players (Canvas, Blackboard) all announced AI grading features
- Student acceptance of AI grading increased dramatically after transparency requirements

**Implications for GradeFlow:**
- Market validation, but increased competition
- Differentiation shifts from "has AI" to "best AI + workflow"
- Trust and transparency become table stakes
- Need to articulate why our AI is better than built-in LMS AI

**Key stat:** 67% of professors say they'd use AI grading if they could review and override suggestions (Educause 2025)

### 2. TA Crisis Intensifies

**What's happening:**
- Graduate student enrollment declining (3rd consecutive year)
- TA wages increasing 15-20% at major universities
- International student TA visa complications
- Unionization accelerating (12 new TA unions formed in 2025)

**Implications for GradeFlow:**
- TA efficiency features becoming more valuable
- "Do more with fewer TAs" is a real pitch
- Department heads now care about TA ROI
- Opportunity: TA training and onboarding features

**Key stat:** Average TA hours per course increased 22% from 2022-2025 while TA availability decreased 15%

### 3. Learning Analytics Maturity

**What's happening:**
- Universities building dedicated learning analytics teams
- Integration requirements increasing (want unified dashboards)
- Predictive analytics for at-risk students now mainstream
- Privacy concerns creating procurement friction

**Implications for GradeFlow:**
- Our analytics are good, not great - area for investment
- Data portability and APIs increasingly important
- FERPA expertise is a differentiator
- Opportunity: "analytics that lead to action" vs. just dashboards

### 4. Department-Level Buying Emerges

**What's happening:**
- Individual professor purchases being consolidated
- Department chairs gaining software budgets
- Standardization pushes from provost offices
- Multi-year contracts becoming norm

**Implications for GradeFlow:**
- Our sales motion needs to evolve
- Department admin features critical
- Cross-course analytics valuable
- Contract negotiation complexity increasing

**Key stat:** 58% of edtech purchases now made at department level or higher (up from 34% in 2023)

### 5. International Expansion Accelerates

**What's happening:**
- Non-US universities modernizing faster
- UK, Australia, Canada leading adoption
- Language and compliance requirements vary significantly
- Currency and pricing complexity

**Implications for GradeFlow:**
- Not yet a priority, but on radar
- Competitors are going international
- GDPR compliance would unlock EU
- Localization is significant investment

## Technology Trends

### AI/ML Specific

**Multimodal grading:**
- Grading handwritten work via image analysis
- Video assignment assessment
- Audio/presentation grading
- GradeFlow status: Not yet, but on radar

**Personalized feedback generation:**
- AI that adapts feedback to student level
- Learning style-aware comments
- Progress-aware suggestions
- GradeFlow status: Exploring for H2 2026

**Plagiarism evolution:**
- Traditional detection becoming less reliable
- AI-generated content detection immature
- "Integrity" framing over "catching cheaters"
- GradeFlow status: Partnership with Turnitin, monitoring

### Infrastructure

**Real-time collaboration:**
- Google Docs-style co-editing expectations
- Synchronous grading sessions
- Live rubric discussions
- GradeFlow status: On roadmap for Q3 2026

**Mobile-first for TAs:**
- TAs want to grade on tablets/phones
- Notification-driven workflows
- Quick actions without full app
- GradeFlow status: Gap - mobile is behind

## Competitor Moves

### Canvas (Instructure)
- Announced "Canvas AI" at InstructureCon 2025
- Basic AI grading in beta
- Heavy investment in analytics
- Positioning as "all-in-one"

**Our response:** Double down on workflow; they can't match our UX in grading-specific features

### Gradescope
- Acquired by Turnitin in 2024
- Expanded beyond STEM
- Better humanities rubrics
- Still weak on TA coordination

**Our response:** TA coordination is our differentiator; they don't have it

### New Entrants
- Several AI-native startups in stealth
- Focus on specific verticals (CS, writing)
- Well-funded but unproven

**Our response:** Speed matters; ship fast, establish network effects

## Regulatory Environment

**Key developments:**
- California AI transparency law (effective 2026) - requires disclosure of AI in grading
- Updated FERPA guidance on AI and student data
- EU AI Act implications for educational AI
- Accessibility requirements increasing (WCAG 2.2)

**GradeFlow status:**
- California law: Compliant, built transparency features in Q4 2025
- FERPA: Compliant, legal review completed
- EU AI Act: Would need work for EU expansion
- Accessibility: Mostly compliant, some gaps in new features

## Market Sizing

**TAM (Total Addressable Market):**
- US higher education: $4.2B for course delivery tools
- Grading/assessment specifically: $1.1B
- Growing 12% annually

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market):**
- US universities using modern LMS: $680M
- Our current penetration: 0.5%

**Realistic 2026 target:**
- Reach 1% penetration = ~$6.8M ARR
- Currently at $3.2M, so 2x growth implied

## Key Takeaways for Product Strategy

1. **AI is necessary but not sufficient** - Everyone will have AI; workflow and UX differentiate
2. **TA features are undervalued** - Competitors ignore this; we should double down
3. **Department sales require department features** - Admin dashboards, cross-course views
4. **Analytics expectations rising** - Our analytics are weak; need investment
5. **Mobile is a gap** - TAs expect mobile; we're behind
6. **International is future, not now** - Monitor but don't invest yet
