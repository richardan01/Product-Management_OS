## Patterns & Learnings

### What consistently works
- Customer-centric KRs (time saved, satisfaction) perform better than feature-shipping KRs
- Conservative targets with stretch → we usually beat them
- Cross-functional OKRs drive better collaboration

### What consistently misses
- Time-to-X metrics (optimistic about reducing friction)
- "Zero bugs" type KRs (always something)
- Anything dependent on external partnerships

### My personal OKR track record
- 85% KR achievement rate over past 4 quarters
- Strongest area: user-facing feature delivery
- Growth area: operational metrics (support tickets, bugs)
