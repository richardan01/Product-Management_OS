# GradeFlow - Company Overview

## Company Facts

- **Founded:** 2022 (San Francisco, CA)
- **Stage:** Series A ($12M raised, led by Reach Capital with participation from GSV Ventures)
- **Employees:** 45 (as of Q1 2026)
- **Valuation:** ~$60M post-money
- **Revenue:** $3.2M ARR (up from $1.1M at Series A)

## Mission & Vision

**Mission:** Give professors their time back so they can focus on what matters - teaching and research.

**Vision:** Every course should feel small, no matter the enrollment.

**Core belief:** The administrative burden of teaching has grown unsustainable. Professors spend 40%+ of their time on grading, coordination, and logistics rather than student interaction.

## Company History

- **2022 Q1:** Founded by Dr. James Liu (former Stanford CS professor) and Rachel Kim (ex-Coursera PM)

## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors

**Canvas (Instructure)** - Market leader LMS
- Strengths: Ubiquity, deep integrations, IT familiarity
- Weaknesses: Bloated, poor UX, slow innovation
- Our angle: We complement Canvas, don't replace it

**Gradescope (Turnitin)** - AI-assisted grading
- Strengths: Strong AI grading for STEM, established in CS departments
- Weaknesses: Narrow use case, no TA coordination, legacy UI
- Our angle: Broader workflow, better for humanities/social sciences

**Slack + Spreadsheets** - The "non-consumption" competitor
- Reality: Most departments cobble together tools
- Our angle: Purpose-built beats general-purpose

### Emerging Competitors

- **Proctorio** - Expanding from proctoring to grading
- **Peergrade** - Peer assessment focus
- **CourseKey** - Attendance expanding to grading

## Company Culture

**Values:**
1. **Professor-first** - Every decision starts with "does this give time back?"
2. **Ship and learn** - Bias toward action, iterate based on feedback
3. **Radical candor** - Direct feedback is caring feedback
4. **One team** - No silos, shared accountability

**Communication norms:**
- All-hands every Monday (30 min)
- Async-first for decisions (Notion)
- Slack for real-time coordination
- In-person for relationship building (hybrid, 2 days/week in SF office)

## Key Risks (Internal)

1. **Concentration risk:** Top 15 universities = 45% of revenue
2. **Seasonality:** Usage drops 60% in summer
3. **AI dependency:** Core AI grading features rely on OpenAI APIs
4. **Churn timing:** Budget decisions happen in spring; we lose deals we don't know about until August
