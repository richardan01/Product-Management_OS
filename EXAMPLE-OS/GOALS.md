**Ownership areas:**

## Q1 2026 Goals

### Goal 1: Reduce grading inconsistency by 40%

**Metric:** Standard deviation in grades across TAs for the same rubric
- **Baseline:** 0.8 std dev (measured Dec 2025)
- **Target:** 0.48 std dev
- **Why it matters:** Inconsistent grading is the #1 complaint from students AND professors. It's also a retention risk - 3 universities cited this in renewal conversations.

**Key initiatives:**
- Calibration exercises before grading opens
- Real-time consistency alerts for TAs
- AI Feedback Assistant (see Goal 4)

**Status:** 🟡 On track but dependent on AI Feedback Assistant timeline

---

### Goal 2: Increase TA adoption to 80%

**Metric:** Weekly active TAs / Total TAs on platform
- **Baseline:** 62% (Jan 2026)
- **Target:** 80%
- **Why it matters:** We can't drive value if TAs aren't using the tool. Low adoption also makes renewals harder - "our TAs don't use it" is a churn reason.

**Key initiatives:**
- Simplified mobile grading (shipped Jan 15)
- TA-specific onboarding flow (in progress)
- Gamification pilot with Stanford (pending)

**Status:** 🟡 Moved from 58% to 62% in Jan. Need to accelerate.

---

### Goal 3: Surface workload imbalances for professors

**Metric:** 90% of professors can identify workload imbalance in 1 click
- **Baseline:** Currently requires 4+ clicks and mental math
- **Target:** Single dashboard view with alerts
- **Why it matters:** Professors don't know which TAs are overloaded until it's too late. This leads to burnout, quality drops, and TA turnover.

**Key initiatives:**
- Workload Dashboard v1 (spec complete, eng starting Feb 10)
- Alert system for >20% variance
- Integration with TA scheduling tool (stretch)

**Status:** 🟢 On track. Prof. Williams wants more scope but I'm holding the line.

---

### Goal 4: Ship AI Feedback Assistant to GA

**Metric:** Available in 100+ courses with <5% rollback rate
- **Baseline:** Currently in beta with 12 courses
- **Target:** GA launch Feb 20, 100 courses by end of Q1
- **Why it matters:** This is the company's biggest bet of the year. AI-assisted feedback could be the moat that differentiates us from competitors.

**Key initiatives:**
- Beta feedback synthesis (complete)
- Hallucination guardrails (in progress)
- Professor override controls (in progress)
- Rollout playbook for CSM team (not started)

**Status:** 🟡 Feb 20 launch is tight. Guardrails work is taking longer than expected.
