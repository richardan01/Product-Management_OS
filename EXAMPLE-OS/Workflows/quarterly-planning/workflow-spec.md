# Quarterly Planning - Workflow Spec

## Purpose

Establish clear priorities for the upcoming quarter that align product work with company goals, are measurable, and have buy-in from leadership and the team.

## Process Overview

```
Week 1:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Step 1: Review Last Quarter (Day 1-2)           │
│     ↓                                           │
│ Step 2: Gather Inputs (Day 3-5)                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Week 2:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Step 3: Draft OKRs (Day 1-2)                    │
│     ↓                                           │
│ Step 4: Review & Finalize (Day 3-5)             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Step 1: Review Last Quarter

See `1-review-last-quarter.md`

**Activities:**
- Score each Key Result (0.0 - 1.0)
- Document what worked and what didn't
- Identify carryover items
- Capture lessons learned

**Output:** Quarter Review document

## Step 2: Gather Inputs

See `2-gather-inputs.md`

**Activities:**
- Collect strategic priorities from CEO
- Understand engineering capacity from CTO
- Review customer feedback and research
- Survey the product team

**Output:** Inputs Summary document

## Step 3: Draft OKRs

See `3-draft-okrs.md`

**Activities:**
- Synthesize inputs into themes
- Draft 3-4 Objectives
- Define measurable Key Results
- Map roadmap items to OKRs

**Output:** Draft OKRs document

## Step 4: Review & Finalize

See `4-review-and-finalize.md`

**Activities:**
- Leadership review meeting
- Incorporate feedback
- Final alignment
- Communicate to team

**Output:** Final OKRs + Roadmap document

## Artifacts Produced

| Artifact | When | Audience |
|----------|------|----------|
| Q[N-1] Review | Week 1 | Leadership + Team |
| Inputs Summary | Week 1 | PM reference |
| Draft OKRs | Week 2 | Leadership review |
| Final OKRs | Week 2 | Company-wide |
| Q[N] Roadmap | Week 2 | Team + Stakeholders |

## Success Criteria

Good quarterly planning results in:
- Clear, measurable objectives
- Team understanding of priorities
- Leadership alignment on direction
- Realistic scope given capacity
- Connection between work and outcomes

## Common Failure Modes

| Failure | Prevention |
|---------|------------|
| Too many objectives | Hard cap at 4 objectives |
| Vague key results | Apply "how will we know?" test |
| No capacity check | Get eng estimate before finalizing |
| Surprise priorities | Gather input early, broadly |
| OKRs ≠ roadmap | Explicitly map roadmap to OKRs |
| No carryover consideration | Review last Q first |
