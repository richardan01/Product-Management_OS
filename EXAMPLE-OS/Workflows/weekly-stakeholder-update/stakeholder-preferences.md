# Stakeholder Preferences

## Primary Recipients

### Sarah Chen (CEO)
**What she cares about:**
- MRR movement and trajectory
- Teacher activation (core growth metric)
- Blockers that need exec help
- Big picture progress vs plan

**Communication style:**
- Loves bullet points
- Wants the "so what" not just the "what"
- Appreciates proactive risk flagging
- Will skim - put important stuff up top

**Pet peeves:**
- Walls of text
- Surprises in all-hands that weren't in updates
- Vague timelines ("soon" vs "by Feb 15")

### James Liu (CTO)
**What he cares about:**
- Technical blockers
- Engineering resource constraints
- System performance and reliability
- Architecture decisions that need alignment

**Communication style:**
- Appreciates technical detail when relevant
- Wants to know if eng help is needed
- Prefers direct problem statements

**Pet peeves:**
- Sugar-coating technical debt
- Not looping in eng early on feasibility

### Priya Sharma (VP Product)
**What she cares about:**
- OKR progress
- Cross-team dependencies
- User research insights
- Roadmap changes

**Communication style:**
- Wants strategic context
- Appreciates connecting work to company goals
- Likes to know what's being learned, not just built

**Pet peeves:**
- Building without user validation
- Scope creep without flagging
- Missing the "why" behind decisions

## Customization by Audience

The weekly update goes to all stakeholders, so balance depth:
- Lead with metrics (Sarah)
- Include technical callouts when relevant (James)
- Connect to OKRs and strategy (Priya)

For deep-dives on specific topics, schedule a separate 1:1 or write a dedicated memo.

## Escalation Triggers

Flag directly to specific stakeholder when:
- **Sarah**: Revenue impact >$5K, major customer escalation
- **James**: Security issue, infrastructure risk, need for >2 eng weeks
- **Priya**: Major roadmap change, competitive threat, strategy pivot needed
