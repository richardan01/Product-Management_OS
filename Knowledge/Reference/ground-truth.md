## Five core criteria

These are applied to every artifact regardless of type.

### Criterion 1 — Structure & Completeness

**My bar:** [What does a complete document look like to you? E.g., "Every required section is present with non-placeholder content. Open questions are listed explicitly, not buried in prose."]

**Shortcuts I dislike:** [E.g., "TBD sections without a date or owner assigned."]

### Criterion 2 — Clarity & Writing Quality

**My bar:** [E.g., "First sentence of every section states the key point. No preamble. No filler phrases. Active voice. Under 80% of the word count a first draft would have."]

**Shortcuts I dislike:** [E.g., "Passive constructions to avoid accountability. 'It was decided that...' instead of 'I decided / Priya decided.'"]

### Criterion 3 — Strategic Alignment ([YOUR_COMPANY])

**My bar:** [E.g., "Every recommendation ties to a metric I own or to a company OKR. Generic industry best practice is not enough."]

**Shortcuts I dislike:** [E.g., "Industry benchmarks cited without adapting to our specific context or stage."]

### Criterion 4 — Actionability

**My bar:** [E.g., "Every recommendation has a named owner, a next action, and a timeframe. 'We should consider...' is not a recommendation."]

**Shortcuts I dislike:** [E.g., "Three-bullet summaries that leave the reader exactly where they started."]

### Criterion 5 — External Reviewer Comments

**My bar:** [E.g., "Every comment from [YOUR_MANAGER] or [HEAD_OF_DEPT] is explicitly addressed — accepted, modified, or rejected with rationale. No comment is silently ignored."]

**N/A condition:** No external reviewer comments detected in the document or conversation.

## Document-type templates

Use these to check required sections during a peer review structural scan.

### PRD

Required sections: Overview / Problem Statement / Goals / Non-Goals / Background / User Stories / Requirements (MH/SH/NTH) / Design / Technical Considerations / Launch Plan / Success Metrics / Open Questions

### Project brief

Required sections: Why we're doing this / Problem statement / Business case / Scope (in/out) / Flight 1 / Success metrics / Risks / Open decisions / Stakeholders / Timeline

### Research summary

Required sections: Research question / Method / Sources / Key findings / Themes / Implications / Open questions / Recommended next step

### Weekly update

Required sections: Shipped / In progress / Blocked / Decisions needed / Next week focus

### 1:1 prep

Required sections: Updates to give / Asks / Decisions / Risks to surface / Follow-ups from last time

## Reviewer shortcuts I always flag

Regardless of document type, a peer review should flag:

- [ ] Placeholder text remaining (`[TBD]`, `INSERT HERE`, `TODO`)
- [ ] Goals stated as activities, not outcomes ("We will build X" instead of "X will achieve Y by Z")
- [ ] Metrics without baselines
- [ ] Risks listed without mitigation or owner
- [ ] External recommendations with no tie to [YOUR_COMPANY] context
- [ ] Action items without a named owner
