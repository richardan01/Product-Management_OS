# Frontier Lab Interview Prep

**Agent:** Henri Ducard (technical depth drilling) + Bruce Wayne (narrative) + Batman (execution week only).

## PD-TOL response scaffold (use for every case)

**Problem → Decision → Tradeoff → Outcome → Learning**

- **Problem:** situation, who was affected, why it mattered (stakes)
- **Decision:** what you chose and why — name 2 alternatives you considered
- **Tradeoff:** what you gave up, what risk you accepted
- **Outcome:** measurable result, time-bound
- **Learning:** what you'd do differently; what generalizes

**[YOUR_NAME]'s anchor cases (fill in your own — don't use placeholders):**
1. [YOUR flagship project / most impressive technical PM case]
2. [A regulated-industry or high-stakes product decision]
3. [A data, infrastructure, or novel-technology case]

## Session workflow

1. **Identify the lab.** Load the cultural fingerprint above.

2. **Run PD-TOL drilling.** Pick one anchor case. Have Henri Ducard run Socratic follow-ups:
   - "Why that decision and not [alternative]?"
   - "What did you give up?"
   - "Show me the number."
   - "What would you do differently?"

3. **"Why [TARGET_COMPANIES]" essay:**
   - 200–400 words
   - Must pass: mission alignment, technical credibility, specific artifact cited, no generic "I'm passionate about AI" framing
   - Riddler mandatory before any version is sent anywhere

4. **Technical depth check.** Before any screen, Henri Ducard runs a spot-check on the technical claims in your prep. Labels each as: *known cold / understood / half-known / faking*. Do not proceed to a screen with any *faking* claims.

5. **Batman activation** (`/cowl-up <interview> <date>`):
   - 96-hour protocol: demo rehearsal × 3 (recorded, watched back) → PD-TOL writeup memorized → 90-min mock with peer → day-of: walk, eat, show up
   - No context-switching once cowl is up

## Anti-patterns (kill on sight)

- "Senior PM with X+ years of experience" — leads with tenure, not signal
- "Passionate about AI" — assumed, not differentiating
- Technical claims without a source → Riddler will block this
- Claiming depth on concepts you're half-known on → Ducard will surface this
